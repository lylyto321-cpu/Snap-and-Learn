## Design Context

### Users
Chinese language learners who photograph text in the wild (signs, textbooks, apps) and want to build vocabulary. They use the app in focused study sessions and quick capture moments. The primary platform is Android mobile.

### Brand Personality
**Calm, intelligent, refined** — a focused study companion that feels trustworthy and well-crafted, not gamified or childish.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Soft & rounded with modern SaaS sensibility (Notion/Linear influence)
- **Emotion**: Calm & focused — zen-like study environment
- **References**: Notion's clean typography and spacing, Linear's subtle shadows and polish
- **Anti-references**: Duolingo's loud gamification, Anki's utilitarian rawness, generic Bootstrap apps
- **Theme**: Light mode only

### Color System
- Primary accent: Teal `#0D9488` (existing — keep, it's calming and scholarly)
- Background: Warm off-white `#F8FAFB`
- Cards: Pure white with very subtle shadows
- Text hierarchy: Dark slate → Medium gray → Light gray
- Danger: Muted red for wrong counts/delete
- Star: Warm amber for favorites

### Typography
- UI text: Inter (already in use — excellent choice)
- Chinese characters: Noto Sans SC (already in use)
- Key principles: Clear hierarchy through weight/size, generous letter-spacing on labels, tight tracking on headings

### Design Principles

1. **Breathing room** — Generous padding and whitespace. Every element should feel like it has space to exist. Never crowd.

2. **Quiet confidence** — Subtle shadows, gentle transitions, muted colors. The app should feel assured, not loud. Animations serve function, not decoration.

3. **Chinese characters are heroes** — The Chinese text should always be the most prominent visual element. It's what the user is learning. Give it size, weight, and presence.

4. **Progressive disclosure** — Show only what's needed. Details expand on tap. Complexity is available but never forced.

5. **Consistent rhythm** — Uniform spacing scale (4px base), consistent border-radius (14px for cards, 12px for buttons, 20px for pills), predictable interaction patterns throughout.
